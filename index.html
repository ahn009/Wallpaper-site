WHAT OUR CUSTOMERS HAVE TO SAY

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is the best way to Install Wallpaper?

The best way to install wallpaper is to prepare the walls properly by cleaning and priming them, measure and cut the wallpaper accurately, use the right adhesive, and smooth out bubbles with a wallpaper brush or squeegee. Always follow the manufacturer's instructions.

Do I need a professional to hang wallpaper?

While DIY wallpapering is possible, hiring a professional ensures a flawless finish, especially with complex patterns or delicate materials. If you're inexperienced, tricky areas like corners and ceilings may be challenging.

Will wallpaper damage walls?

Wallpaper itself doesn‚Äôt damage walls if removed correctly. However, improper removal or low-quality adhesive can peel off paint or drywall. Using a steamer or wallpaper removal solution helps minimize damage.

Which type of wallpaper is best?

The best type depends on the room: Vinyl is durable and washable (great for kitchens/bathrooms), Fiberglass is breathable and mold-resistant, while Non-woven is easy to install and remove.

What is the most expensive type of wallpaper?

Handmade or custom-designed wallpapers (like silk, grasscloth, or metallic finishes) are typically the most expensive due to their premium materials and intricate craftsmanship.

What type of wallpaper makes a room look bigger?

Light-colored wallpapers with small, subtle patterns or vertical stripes create an illusion of space. Glossy finishes also reflect light, making rooms appear larger.

What is the most trending wallpaper?

Current trends include botanical prints, geometric designs, textured finishes (like faux concrete), and bold murals. Peel-and-stick wallpapers are also popular for renters.

Can I wallpaper over painted walls?

Yes, but the walls must be clean, smooth, and free of flaking paint. A primer is recommended to ensure proper adhesion, especially for glossy surfaces.

How do I fix bubbles after wallpaper is dry?

Use a small syringe to inject adhesive under the bubble, then gently press it flat with a roller. For stubborn bubbles, a small pin prick can release trapped air before smoothing.

Is wallpaper safe for children's rooms?

Most modern wallpapers are non-toxic, but opt for vinyl or washable finishes for easy cleaning. Avoid heavy embossing where kids might pick at the edges.
